Loyal Citizen

In-Game Description:

Loyal citizens are people who survived the Seven Hour War, and were relocated to City 17 to be forced into a form of slavery. Every loyal citizens top priority is to do what the Combine tells them to do. Local citizens are, as you might expect, loyal to the Combine and will do what they are told, so they are not permitted to craft weapons and fight the Combine.


Duties:

  • Listen to metrocops and Earth's Administrator for your daily duties. If you aren't told to do anything, ask a member of the Combine and they will give you orders.

  • Take your food supplies every day from the plaza food distributor.

  • Help the Combine search for potential rebels throughout the city.

  • Help the Combine by mining crafting materials for them.

Special Job-Specific Items:

  • None

Job-Specific Rules/Tips:

  • You are not permitted to kill as this job unless it is self defense.

  • Don't enter places you know you aren't allowed to enter, or else you may be killed or arrested.

Refugee

In-Game Description:

Refugees are civilians of City 17 who have decided to fight against the combine. They typically create bases in areas of the city that are inaccessible to normal citizens to avoid detection.


Duties:

  • Fight against the Combine.

Special Job-Specific Items:

  • None

Job-Specific Rules/Tips:

  • Don't kill other refugees or rebels unless they are betraying the rebellion.

  • Don't kill civilians unless you know they are helping the combine fight you in some way.

Resistance Leader

In-Game Description:

The resistance leader is in charge of commanding the rebel force. It is their duty to recruit any refugees who are looking to fight the combine. It is recommended that you say hidden indoors and underground. Since you are one of the highest ranking members of the resistance, you cannot afford to get yourself captured and forced by the combine to give out resistance intel.


Duties:

  • Recruit refugees who are looking to rebel against the combine.

  • Stay out of sight of the Combine or you may be captured or killed.

Special Job-Specific Items:

  • Has access to a SMG for defending refugees as well as a disguise kit.

  • Has ability to command refugees.

Job-Specific Rules/Tips:

  • It's highly recommended that you stay out of sight of the combine to avoid being captured. If you do have to go outside though, make sure you are secured by plenty of refugees or at least have a disguise on.

  • To combine jobs: you cannot kill a disguised resistance leader dealer unless he/she has a rebel or leader playermodel. (Examples of leader models: Eli, Alyx, Mossman, Barney, Odessa)

Scientist

In-Game Description:

As a scientist, you spawn inside the jails of the citadel. Your task is to follow any orders the Combine gives you and help them research advanced weapons and units through the science locker. If and when the rebels free you from the Combine, your task is then to help the rebels get to the top of the citadel to activate the detonation codes.


Duties:

  • Help the Combine develop advanced weapons and soldier units. (When imprisoned)

  • Help the resistance destroy the citadel by entering the detonation codes. (Once freed and able to storm the citadel)

Special Job-Specific Items:

  • Has access to the Combine science locker.

  • Has ability to activate the citadel detonation codes.

Job-Specific Rules:

  • You must follow all orders from the Combine. Failure to do so may lead to them killing you, giving the resistance less of a chance of victory.

  • It's recommended that you have a rebel escort up to the detonation codes to avoid dying before they can be activated.

Vortigaunt

In-Game Description:

The vortigaunt is an alien species that has been enslaved by the Combine alongside humanity. Vorts are able to shoot fatal amounts of electricity from their hands and are very strong, allowing them to do things faster and easier compared to humans. By default, Vorts are under the rule of the Combine, but can be put under rebel command if given the opportunity.


Duties:

  • Follow orders from either the Combine or rebels.

Special Job-Specific Items:

  • Has ability to shoot lightning as well as mine rocks faster than humans.

Job-Specific Rules:

  • You must follow all orders from whoever is commanding you. By default, it's going to be the Combine.

  • You can choose to be independent or try to work for both sides but only if you're relieved of your duties by whichever side is commanding you.

Government Man

In-Game Description:

The Government Man for City 17 RP works like a spy. He can choose a side (combine or rebels) and help them by giving them advantages such as hidden base locations and attack plans. The Government Man can take two sides at once if he pleases. Players can turn on the Government Man if they have reason to believe he is a spy for the enemy, but they must have solid evidence before killing.


Duties:

  • Give either the Combine, Rebels, or both teams advantages by giving away secrets and base locations.

Special Job-Specific Items:

  • Has access to entire map under certain circumstances.

  • Has ability to teleport back and forth between two points.

Job-Specific Rules:

  • If you decide to help out both sides, try not to give away too much info about each side; it might result in you being killed for spying.

  • Do not abuse the teleportation system in any way.

Metrocop

In-Game Description:

Civil Protection is essentially the Combine's thought police in all urban areas on Earth, including City 17. Part of the Combine Overwatch, CPs are ordinary human volunteers who have "willingly" joined the Combine, either for more privileges, such as additional food, better living conditions, an increase in authority and status over others, or out of genuine sympathy and identification with the Combine's aims. As such, they are not bio-mechanically modified in any way, unlike the two other Overwatch units.


Duties:

  • Keep the city safe from any threats.

  • Patrol certain sectors of the city.

  • Set up checkpoints in high traffic areas so citizens have to show an ID before passing through.

Special Job-Specific Items:

  • Has access to sidearms and SMGs.

  • Has ability to setup checkpoints as well as patrol wherever they please, unless otherwise assigned a specific sector by the Earth Admin.

Job-Specific Rules/Tips:

  • Don't discharge your weapons unless it is REALLY necessary.

  • You can prop block for checkpoints, but you still need to let players through if you know they live in that certain sector. Don't think it's ok to not let someone through.

  • Ask permission from either the overwatch commander or a server admin before setting up a non-permanent checkpoint. This is only in place so players don't set up checkpoints in areas that shouldn't have one.

  • You are not allowed to patrol in areas that you know players like to hide out. Doing so would be considered metagaming and could result in a temporary job ban. This includes checking out the hidden rebel base and the sewers because you think rebels are hiding there. In other words, you need solid evidence that rebels are currently hiding there before you move in to raid.

Overwatch Soldier

In-Game Description:

Overwatch Soldiers are the basic trans-human infantry units of the Combine Overwatch, composing the backbone of the Combine's military presence on Earth. In contrast to Civil Protection, whose duties are to enforce order within suppressed population centers, soldiers of the Overwatch are tasked with more hazardous actions requiring skill and tact: from patrolling the treacherous borders of Combine-controlled territory to raiding Resistance-held strongholds.


Duties:

  • Guard the citadel walls and any other possible entrances to the citadel.

  • Help raid known rebel bases.

  • Assist metrocops in firefights.

Special Job-Specific Items:

  • Has ability to be in the front line with metrocops when fighting rebels.

  • Has access to the Combine SMG and grenades as well as access to certain parts of the citadel.

Job-Specific Rules/Jobs:

  • Don't go to restricted parts of the citadel without permission from Earth's Administrator.

  • Your duty is not to patrol, so do not go patrolling with metrocops.

Overwatch Guard

In-Game Description:

The Overwatch Guard is in charge of guarding and maintaining the Citadel and any other places that may have a major Overwatch presence. Guards can also use Combine sniper rifles and stand guard on top of a building.


Duties:

  • Protect restricted areas mainly in the citadel.

  • Report any suspicious activity that goes on to Earth's Administrator.

Special Job-Specific Items:

  • Has access to the Combine sniper rifle and certain areas of the citadel.

Job-Specific Rules/Tips:

  • Don't go to restricted parts of the citadel without permission from Earth's Administrator.

Overwatch Elite

In-Game Description:

Elites are tougher, achieve better accuracy with their weapons, and inflict more overall damage than regular soldiers do. They typically carry Overwatch Standard Issue Pulse Rifles, and are able to use the weapon's secondary fire Energy Ball. They use this advantage without hesitation and with deadly accuracy. Elites also may be picked to protect the Earth's Admin's office.


Duties:

  • Assist Overwatch Soldiers in firefights if needed.

  • Protect Earth's Administrator's office if he requests it.

Special Job-Specific Items:

  • Has access to weapon shipments and restricted areas of the citadel.

  • Has ability to set up official Combine bases/hideouts for easy access to possible rebel activity.

Job-Specific Rules/Tips:

  • You are permitted to take the role of the Earth Admin if there is currently no Earth Admin present and there are 5 or less players on the server.

  • ONLY discharge your weapons if you see a threat. The only exception is target practice in the citadel prison.

  • Don't go to restricted parts of the citadel without permission from Earth's Administrator.

  • Patrolling is not your job. Only go into the streets if you are ordered to.

  • Only set up Combine bases/hideouts close to areas that you know have rebel activity.

  • Only use your weapon shipments at official Combine bases, nowhere else.

Earth's Administrator

Note: For a list of commands that go along with this job, click here.

In-Game Descripton:

Earth's Administrator is the former head of Black Mesa, and later named Earth's Admin under the Combine. From his headquarters on Earth in the Citadel of City 17, he is humanity's representative within the Combine.


Duties:

  • Keep City 17 in order and regularly have meetings with Combine forces.

  • Make city-wide alert announcements if necessary.

In the Event of an Emergency:

If something happens and action need to be taken, there are 3 things you can do as Earth's Administrator. You can either 1) Call a judgment waiver, 2) Call an autonomous waiver, or 3) Call a city lockdown.


Special Job-Specific Items:

  • Has access to entire city, including Combine bases.

  • Has ability to control the entire Combine force as well as spawn weapon shipments.

Job-Specific Rules/Tips:

  • Do not leave the citadel without an escort.

  • Only use your weapon shipments in certain areas of the Citadel.

  • You cannot use weapons for self defense if there are 4 or more combine units on the server.

  • You can only deploy soldiers, guards, and elites if the situation calls for any of them. For example, don't send out elites to patrol early in the game and rebels have even shown activity yet, and don't send out soldiers to raid an apartment for a small violation. Use metrocops to patrol and raid until the rebels have started to become an effective resistance.
